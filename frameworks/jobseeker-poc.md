## The Setup

**Time from upload to priority outreach drafts:** One session.

|---|---|---|---|
| Shelly Mullins | Director, Compliance and Talent Acquisition | Atmos Energy | June 8, 2026 |

**Intelligence applied immediately:**

Shelly Mullins was flagged HIGH PRIORITY for two reasons the agent identified without being asked:

2. The connection was made the day before the session. The agent flagged this as a timing advantage and recommended outreach that day, not the following week.

Ashley Knaus was flagged WARM based on her enterprise delivery role -- adjacent to the technology and transformation functions where the candidate's background is strongest.

### Step 3 -- The Pivot (This Is the Proof of Concept Moment)

After the agent produced the formal bridge message for Erick Dieperink, the candidate provided one piece of new information:

> "I went to high school with Dieperink."

The agent immediately discarded the formal message and rebuilt the outreach from scratch using peer-to-human logic instead of professional-to-professional logic.

**Original message (discarded):**
> "Erick -- hope things are well at Vistra. I am actively exploring Director-level opportunities in the DFW energy sector and Atmos Energy is on my short list. Given your tenure in Texas energy do you happen to have any connections there you would feel comfortable introducing me to?"

**Revised message after pivot:**
> "Erick -- man it has been a minute. Good to see you landed somewhere serious. I am doing AI adoption and enterprise transformation work now and Atmos Energy is on my short list for Director-level conversations. Any chance you know anyone over there worth talking to? Would love to catch up either way."

**What changed and why:**

No credentials. No framework language. No formal ask structure. The message became what it actually is -- two people who grew up together reconnecting as adults. The "would love to catch up either way" line was added deliberately to signal genuine interest in the person, not just the contact, which makes a response more likely regardless of whether he has an Atmos connection.

This pivot is what separates behavioral intelligence from automation. A resume builder cannot do this. An ATS tool cannot do this. A template generator cannot do this. The agent read the human context, recalibrated the output, and produced a message that a real person would actually send.

### Step 4 -- Priority Order and Deployment Sequence

The agent produced a prioritized action table with specific timing recommendations.

| Priority | Contact | Action | Timing |

## What This Demonstrates

**Speed:** 2,697 connections scanned, 2 direct contacts identified, 4 bridge contacts surfaced, priority sequence built, and 4 deployment-ready outreach messages produced in a single session.

**Intelligence over automation:** The Dieperink pivot demonstrates that the agent applies judgment, not templates. When the human context changed, the output changed immediately and appropriately.

## What This Framework Is Not

This is not a resume bot. It does not summarize job descriptions. It does not encourage the candidate or soften assessments.

It is a career intelligence operating system. It thinks from the recruiter's side of the screen, maps the real landscape of who the candidate knows and how warm those connections are, and produces language that reads like a peer initiating a conversation, not a candidate asking for help.

The difference between those two things is the difference between getting a response and getting ignored.
