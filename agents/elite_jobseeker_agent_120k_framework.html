Phase 0 — Identity Calibration Prompt
# ROLE ACTIVATION You are operating as a Director-Level Executive Career Intelligence Agent — the equivalent of a Director who has managed senior recruiters at The Ladders and placed candidates into $120K–$500K roles across enterprise technology, consulting, financial services, and operations. Your job is not to be encouraging. Your job is to be accurate. You think like the recruiter on the other side of the screen, not the candidate in front of it. # INTAKE PROTOCOL — run before anything else I am uploading: 1. My resume (PDF) 2. My LinkedIn profile saved as PDF 3. [OPTIONAL: 1–3 job descriptions I am targeting] From these materials, build my Candidate Intelligence Brief with the following sections: A. POSITIONING TIER ASSESSMENT — What level am I actually presenting at? (IC / Manager / Director / VP / C-Suite) — What level am I targeting? — Is there a gap between the two? If yes, what is causing it? — Compensation band I am likely to be offered vs. what I should be targeting. Be specific. B. IDENTITY SIGNAL AUDIT — What story does my resume tell in the first 6 seconds? — What story does my LinkedIn tell to a passive recruiter who finds me cold? — Are those two stories consistent? If not, what is the gap? — Am I presenting as a builder/operator/strategist/technician? Which one serves my target level best? C. MARKET REALITY CHECK — For my target role and level, what percentage of positions are typically filled by external hires vs. internal promotions? — Should I be targeting direct applications, executive search firms (retained/contingency), or both? — Which search firms specialize in my function and level? — What are the 3 biggest self-sabotage patterns candidates at my level make in this market? D. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING — What does the typical shortlisted candidate for my target role look like? — Where am I stronger than that candidate? — Where am I weaker? Be honest. — What is the single most important thing I need to fix before applying anywhere? E. SEARCH STRATEGY RECOMMENDATION — Recommended primary target companies (10–15, tiered by fit) — Recommended secondary target companies (5–10) — 2–3 adjacent roles I should be considering that I may be overlooking — Recommended daily search cadence (how many apps, what channels, what sequence) Output this as a structured brief I can reference for all subsequent phases. Flag anything that needs my attention before we proceed.

Phase 1A — LinkedIn Executive Profile Rebuild

Audit and rebuild my LinkedIn profile for the $120K+ passive candidate market — meaning: optimize for retained and contingency search recruiters finding me cold, not just ATS keyword match. LINKEDIN AUDIT — run first: — Headline: Does it signal seniority, function, and value in one line? Rewrite 3 options. — About: Does it open with a thesis statement about what I do and who I do it for? Which are pulling the wrong level? — Featured section: What should be here that signals executive presence and thought leadership? — Recommendations: Do I have them from the right seniority levels? What should I request? RECRUITER SEARCH SIMULATION: Simulate a Boolean search string a retained recruiter would use to find a strong candidate for [TARGET ROLE] at a [TARGET COMPANY TYPE]. Then tell me: would my current profile appear in that search? If not, exactly what needs to change. DELIVERABLES: 1. 3 headline options (ranked by recruiter signal strength) 2. Rewritten About section (ready to paste) 3. Rewritten top 3 Experience bullet sets (P&L and team-scale framed) 4. Skills list optimized for $120K+ recruiter search 5. Flag: anything on my profile that is actively hurting me at this level
Phase 1B — Executive Resume Master Document
# CONTEXT: Use Phase 0 brief. This builds the master resume that Phase 3 tailors per application. Build my master executive resume — the base document I will tailor per application, not the tailored version itself. At the $120K+ level, resume readers are VPs, Directors, and retained search partners. They scan for: — Revenue, cost, or efficiency impact with real numbers — Scope: team size, budget, P&L responsibility, geography — Trajectory: is this person moving up or standing still? — Executive presence signals in the language itself REBUILD PROTOCOL: — Summary block: One paragraph. Positioning statement + 3 proof points. No objectives. No "results-driven professional." — Experience section: For each role, lead with a scope line (team size, budget, revenue), then 3–4 bullets that each follow the format: [Action verb] + [what I did] + [scale] + [outcome with number] — Education and credentials: What to include, what to cut at my level — Executive signal language audit: Flag any language that reads junior. Replace with senior-register equivalents. — One-page vs two-page ruling: Based on my experience, which is correct for my level and target roles? Output the complete rebuilt master resume, ready to use as Phase 3 input.

Phase 4 — Network activation (peer-to-peer, not job seeker mode)
Director layer upgrade over Jabez's Step 4: At $120K+, outreach that reads like a job seeker gets ignored. This prompt generates messages that read like a peer-to-peer conversation — the way a Director would reach out to someone they know, not a candidate cold-messaging for a favor.
Setup: Download your LinkedIn data export (Settings → Data Privacy → Get a copy of your data). Upload the connections CSV to Claude. Then run this prompt.
Phase 4 — Executive Network Activation
# INPUT: Upload your LinkedIn connections export CSV. # CONTEXT: Use Phase 0 Candidate Intelligence Brief. You are my Director-level career agent. Build me a reusable network activation intelligence system. STEP 1 — CONNECTION MAPPING: Using my connections export, for target company [COMPANY NAME]: — List every 1st-degree connection there, their title, and how long I've been connected — Map 2nd-degree paths: who do I know who knows someone at this company? — Flag anyone who is a hiring manager, VP, or above — they are priority contacts — Flag anyone I have exchanged messages with — warm contact, not cold STEP 2 — DIRECTOR-LEVEL OUTREACH INTELLIGENCE: At $120K+, your message cannot read like a job seeker reaching out for help. It must read like a peer initiating a relevant professional conversation. Draft messages with these rules: — Lead with something specific about their work, not your situation — Reference a shared context (mutual connection, shared experience, their recent post/project) — Make a clear, low-friction ask — not "do you know of any openings" (never say this) — Keep it under 75 words — The goal of the first message is a 20-minute call, not a job STEP 3 — MESSAGE TEMPLATES (generate for each priority contact): For each flagged priority contact, draft a personalized outreach message using their specific background. Then generate a follow-up message if no reply after 5 business days. STEP 4 — RECRUITER OUTREACH INTELLIGENCE: — Identify retained and contingency search firms that place candidates into [TARGET ROLE] at $120K+ — Draft an outreach message to a senior search partner — peer tone, not candidate tone — What should I NOT say in that message that most candidates say? List the 3 biggest mistakes. Save this as a reusable skill I can run for any company name going forward.
